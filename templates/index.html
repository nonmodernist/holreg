Hollywood Regionalism

Film Adaptations of American Women's Regional Literature, 1910-1961

Between 1910 and 1961, the United States film industry made over 100 films adapted from the novels and short stories of American women regionalists. This digital archive explores how Hollywood engaged with regional literature and the nation-building project of American literary regionalism.

About This Project

This research explores how the Hollywood studio system appropriated successful brands of regionalism and their audiences while also appropriating and augmenting particular modes of environmental and social activism. The films examined here demonstrate both the persistence and the problems of American literary regionalism as it transitioned from print to screen.
